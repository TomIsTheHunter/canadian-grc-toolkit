## 1. Overview — Three Acts in One Bill

Bill C-27 is an omnibus bill comprising **three separate Acts**:

| Act                                                                | Purpose                                                                                                                               |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Consumer Privacy Protection Act (CPPA)**                         | Replaces PIPEDA for the private sector; enhanced individual privacy rights, consent requirements, and breach notification obligations |
| **Personal Information and Data Protection Tribunal Act (PIDPTA)** | Creates a new administrative tribunal to adjudicate CPPA complaints and levy financial penalties                                      |
| **Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (AIDA)**                    | First Canadian federal AI governance legislation; requirements for high-impact AI systems                                             |

### Relationship to PIPEDA

If enacted, the **CPPA would repeal and replace PIPEDA** as the primary federal private-sector privacy law. FRFIs currently subject to PIPEDA would transition to CPPA obligations.

## 2. Consumer Privacy Protection Act (CPPA)

### 2.1 Key Enhancements Over PIPEDA

| Area                    | PIPEDA                              | CPPA (Proposed)                                                                                         |
|-------------------------|-------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Consent**             | Meaningful consent                  | Enhanced consent requirements; implied consent more restricted; right to withdraw                       |
| **Individual Rights**   | Limited rights                      | Right to access, correction, portability, deletion (de-indexing), and to object                         |
| **Accountability**      | Privacy policies                    | Enhanced accountability; Privacy Management Programme required                                          |
| **Breach Notification** | SOR/2018-64 (PIPEDA DERR)           | Strengthened breach notification; similar "real risk of significant harm" threshold with expanded scope |
| **Children's Privacy**  | Limited specific provisions         | Enhanced protection for minors                                                                          |
| **Enforcement**         | OPC recommendations → Federal Court | New tribunal with direct penalty powers                                                                 |

### 2.2 Privacy Management Programme

Under the CPPA, FRFIs must maintain a **Privacy Management Programme** covering:
- Privacy policies and procedures
- Individual rights request management
- Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA) requirements
- Training and accountability structures
- Breach management and notification procedures

### 2.3 Individual Rights

| Right                      | Description                                                                   |
|----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Access**                 | Individuals can request copies of their personal information held by the FRFI |
| **Correction**             | Individuals can request correction of inaccurate information                  |
| **Portability**            | Right to receive personal information in a portable format                    |
| **Deletion / De-indexing** | Right to deletion in specified circumstances                                  |
| **Object**                 | Right to object to certain processing activities                              |

## 3. Personal Information and Data Protection Tribunal Act (PIDPTA)

### 3.1 New Tribunal Structure

The PIDPTA creates the **Personal Information and Data Protection Tribunal** — an administrative tribunal with power to:
- Hear appeals of Privacy Commissioner (OPC) decisions
- Levy **financial penalties** for CPPA violations (up to **5% of global annual revenue or CAD $25 million**, whichever is greater, for the most serious violations)
- Award damages to individuals for privacy harm

### 3.2 Penalty Tiers

| Tier                | Maximum Penalty                  | Applicable Violations                                                           |
|---------------------|----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Tier 1 (higher)** | 5% of global revenue or CAD $25M | Most serious violations — e.g., failure to report breach, knowing contravention |
| **Tier 2 (lower)**  | 3% of global revenue or CAD $10M | Less serious violations — e.g., administrative non-compliance                   |

## 4. Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (AIDA)

### 4.1 Purpose

AIDA is Canada's proposed framework for governing **high-impact AI systems** — AI systems that pose significant risk to health, safety, or individual rights. AIDA would require:

- Identification and classification of high-impact AI systems
- Risk assessment and mitigation for high-impact systems
- Mandatory impact assessments
- Incident reporting obligations for harms caused by AI systems

### 4.2 High-Impact AI Systems in Financial Services

For FRFIs, the following AI systems may be designated as "high-impact" under AIDA:
- Automated lending decisions (credit adjudication)
- Anti-money laundering (AML) and fraud detection models
- Customer risk scoring systems
- Algorithmic trading systems

### 4.3 AIDA Obligations (If Enacted)

| Obligation             | Description                                                            |
|------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Risk assessment**    | Assess and document risks for high-impact AI systems before deployment |
| **Mitigation**         | Implement measures to mitigate risks of biased output or harm          |
| **Monitoring**         | Ongoing monitoring of high-impact system performance                   |
| **Record-keeping**     | Document risk assessments, decisions, and incidents                    |
| **Incident reporting** | Report harms caused by high-impact AI systems                          |

## 5. Breach Reporting Obligations Under CPPA

### 5.1 Reportable Breach Definition

Under the CPPA, a **breach of security safeguards** must be reported when it creates a "real risk of significant harm" to affected individuals. The definition of harm is proposed to be expanded relative to PIPEDA DERR.

### 5.2 Notification Requirements

| Notification                | Recipient                                        | Timing                                                               |
|-----------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **OPC Report**              | Office of the Privacy Commissioner               | As soon as feasible after breach is determined to meet the threshold |
| **Individual Notification** | All individuals at real risk of significant harm | As soon as feasible                                                  |
| **Affected Organisations**  | Other organisations that may mitigate harm       | Concurrent with individual notification                              |

### 5.3 Breach Records

FRFIs must maintain records of all security breaches, regardless of whether they meet the reporting threshold. Records must be retained for the prescribed period (expected to mirror PIPEDA DERR's 24-month retention requirement).

## 6. Enforcement and Penalties

### 6.1 Privacy Commissioner Powers

Under the CPPA, the OPC's powers would be strengthened:
- Power to issue compliance orders (vs. recommendations under PIPEDA)
- Power to initiate investigations on own initiative
- Power to refer matters to the Tribunal for penalty hearings

### 6.2 Tribunal Powers

The Tribunal can levy penalties per Section 3 above. The penalty regime is among the most significant proposed changes — moving Canada's privacy enforcement environment substantially closer to the GDPR model.

## 7. Interaction with OSFI Obligations

If enacted, the CPPA would create **concurrent and intersecting obligations** with OSFI's regulatory framework for FRFIs:

| Interaction Area                        | Description                                                                                                                                                                        |
|-----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Breach notification timing**          | CPPA breach reporting to OPC ("as soon as feasible") aligns with OSFI Advisory Criterion C3 — notification to the OPC is an OSFI incident reporting trigger                        |
| **Privacy Management Programme ↔ B-13** | The CPPA Privacy Management Programme intersects with B-13 governance requirements; breach response procedures must be integrated                                                  |
| **AIDA ↔ E-23**                         | AIDA high-impact AI governance would complement and potentially interact with OSFI E-23 model risk management                                                                      |
| **Penalty exposure**                    | CPPA penalties (up to 5% global revenue) are a material financial risk that would typically require Board and Senior Management escalation — triggering OSFI Advisory Criterion C2 |
| **Individual rights requests**          | Data access and deletion requests may require FRFI systems to locate and handle personal data — operational implications for technology and data management (B-13)                 |

## 8. Relevance to OSFI 24-Hour Incident Reporting

### 8.1 OPC Notification as an OSFI Trigger

Under the current PIPEDA DERR framework (and the proposed CPPA), notification to the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada following a breach is a well-established practice. Under the OSFI Advisory **Criterion C3**, notification to the OPC (or any other domestic or foreign regulatory or supervisory agency) is a **standalone OSFI incident reporting trigger**.

This means:
- If the FRFI determines a breach requires OPC notification → OSFI must be notified simultaneously or earlier
- Under CPPA, the reporting threshold may be broadened, potentially **increasing the number of incidents that concurrently trigger both CPPA and OSFI reporting**

### 8.2 Expanded Breach Scope Increases Concurrent Reporting Scenarios

The CPPA's proposed expansion of the breach reporting scope (relative to PIPEDA DERR) means more incidents may require OPC notification. Since OPC notification triggers OSFI Advisory Criterion C3, CPPA implementation would likely **increase the volume of incidents requiring concurrent OSFI reporting**.

### 8.3 AIDA Incident Reporting Overlaps with OSFI Advisory

If AIDA is enacted, AI system incidents causing harm would require reporting under AIDA in addition to potential reporting under the OSFI Advisory. FRFIs must build an **integrated incident classification process** that simultaneously assesses incidents against:
- OSFI Advisory trigger criteria (24-hour window)
- CPPA breach reporting threshold (concurrent OPC notification)
- AIDA incident reporting obligations
- Quebec Law 25 CAI notification obligations

> 📌 **Summary:** Bill C-27, if enacted, would create strengthened and expanded breach notification obligations that run concurrently with OSFI incident reporting. The OPC notification obligation under the CPPA is itself an OSFI trigger criterion (C3). FRFIs should monitor legislative developments and prepare integrated compliance processes.
