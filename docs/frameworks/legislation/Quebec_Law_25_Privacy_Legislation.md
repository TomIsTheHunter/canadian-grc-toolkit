## 1. Overview

**Quebec Law 25** (officially *An Act to modernise legislative provisions as regards the protection of personal information*, also known as Bill 64) is a comprehensive reform of Quebec's private-sector privacy law. It amends the **Act respecting the protection of personal information in the private sector (ARPPIPS)** and the *Act respecting Access to documents held by public bodies and the Protection of personal information*.

Law 25 significantly strengthens privacy protections in Quebec, bringing them closer in alignment with **GDPR** principles and creating obligations that in several respects exceed federal PIPEDA requirements.

### Why Law 25 Matters for FRFIs

FRFIs operating in Quebec or holding personal information of Quebec residents must comply with both **PIPEDA** (federal) and **Law 25** (provincial). Where obligations differ, FRFIs must satisfy **the more stringent standard**.

The most significant difference from PIPEDA is the **72-hour reporting window** to the Commission d'accès à l'information (CAI) — substantially stricter than PIPEDA's "as soon as feasible" standard.

## 2. Phased Implementation

Law 25 was implemented in three phases:

| Phase       | Date               | Key Changes                                                                                                                                                                       |
|-------------|--------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Phase 1** | September 22, 2022 | Privacy Officer designation; breach notification obligations (72-hour CAI reporting, individual notification); privacy incident register                                          |
| **Phase 2** | September 22, 2023 | Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs) for new projects; enhanced consent requirements; automated decision-making disclosure; right to data portability; biometric database regulation |
| **Phase 3** | September 22, 2023 | Remaining provisions fully in force                                                                                                                                               |

> 📌 **As of April 2, 2026:** Law 25 is **fully in force** — all three phases have been implemented.

## 3. Scope — Who Must Comply

Law 25 applies to any **enterprise** (private or public sector) that **collects, holds, uses, or discloses personal information in the course of carrying on an enterprise** in Quebec. This includes:

- FRFIs with physical operations in Quebec
- FRFIs that service Quebec-resident customers (even without physical presence in Quebec)
- Third-party service providers processing personal information on behalf of a FRFI in Quebec

The **Commission d'accès à l'information (CAI)** is the enforcement authority.

## 4. Confidentiality Incident Reporting — CAI 72-Hour Rule

### 4.1 Definition of a Confidentiality Incident

Under Law 25, a **confidentiality incident** is:
- **Access** to personal information not authorised by law
- **Use** of personal information not authorised by law
- **Communication** of personal information not authorised by law
- **Loss** of personal information (loss of control over the information)

This definition is broad and functionally similar to PIPEDA's "breach of security safeguards."

### 4.2 The Reporting Threshold

A confidentiality incident must be reported to the CAI and affected individuals when it presents a **risk of serious injury** to a person.

Factors for assessing risk of serious injury include:
- Sensitivity of the personal information involved
- Anticipated consequences (discrimination, harassment, identity theft, financial loss)
- Probability of misuse

### 4.3 The 72-Hour CAI Reporting Window

> 📌 **Law 25 CAI Notification Window: 72 hours from the moment the organisation becomes aware of the incident.**

This is significantly stricter than the federal PIPEDA DERR standard ("as soon as feasible") and creates a distinct, binding deadline for Quebec incidents.

| Obligation                  | Timeline                                                                                  |
|-----------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **CAI Notification**        | Within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the incident                                     |
| **Individual Notification** | As soon as possible once the organisation has taken measures to reduce the risk of injury |

### 4.4 Content of the CAI Report

The CAI notification must include:
- Date of the incident and date it was discovered
- Nature of the personal information involved
- Number of persons affected (or estimated)
- Description of how the incident occurred
- Measures taken or planned to reduce risk of injury
- Contact information for the Privacy Officer

---

## 5. Individual Notification Obligations

Affected individuals must be notified when a confidentiality incident presents a risk of serious injury. Notification must:
- Be made directly to the affected individuals (not only through a public notice)
- Include sufficient information to allow the individual to take protective measures
- Be made **as soon as possible** after implementing protective measures

Content of individual notification:
- Description of the incident
- Type of information involved
- Steps the individual can take to reduce the risk of harm
- Organisation's contact information
- Individual's right to file a complaint with the CAI

## 6. Confidentiality Incident Register

### 6.1 Register Requirement

Every confidentiality incident — **regardless of whether it meets the reporting threshold** — must be recorded in a **Confidentiality Incident Register**.

This register obligation parallels PIPEDA DERR's record-keeping requirement and is similarly broad.

### 6.2 Register Contents

| Field                         | Description                                                       |
|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Date of discovery             | When the organisation became aware of the incident                |
| Nature of the incident        | How it occurred                                                   |
| Personal information involved | Type, categories, and sensitivity                                 |
| Number of persons affected    | Actual or estimated                                               |
| Risk assessment               | Whether risk of serious injury was identified                     |
| Notification decisions        | CAI and individual notification details (or reasons not required) |
| Mitigation actions            | Steps taken to address the incident                               |

### 6.3 CAI Access to Register

The CAI may request access to the register at any time. Organisations must produce the register upon request.

## 7. Privacy Impact Assessments (PIAs)

### 7.1 When PIAs Are Required

Under Law 25 Phase 2 (September 2023), **Privacy Impact Assessments are mandatory** before:
- Acquiring, developing, or overhauling an information system or electronic service delivery system
- Communicating personal information outside Quebec
- Entering into a service agreement with a third party that will involve personal information

### 7.2 PIA Contents

A Privacy Impact Assessment must:
- Identify and assess privacy risks associated with the project
- Propose measures to mitigate identified risks
- Be proportionate to the sensitivity of the information and the scale of the project
- Be documented and available for CAI review

### 7.3 Cross-Border Data Transfers

Before communicating personal information outside Quebec, organisations must conduct a PIA to verify that the receiving jurisdiction provides adequate protection. This is directly relevant to FRFIs using cloud services or third-party vendors with international data flows.

---

## 8. Privacy Officer Designation

Law 25 requires every organisation to designate a **Privacy Officer** (or person responsible for personal information protection):

- The Privacy Officer is responsible for ensuring the organisation's compliance with Law 25
- The role may be held by the CEO by default (in the absence of a specific designation)
- Large organisations should designate a qualified person with resources to fulfil the role
- The Privacy Officer's name and contact information must be published on the organisation's website

For FRFIs, the Privacy Officer role typically aligns with the Chief Privacy Officer (CPO) or the DPO-equivalent role under PIPEDA.

## 9. Consent and Individual Rights

### 9.1 Enhanced Consent Requirements

Law 25 requires **express, explicit consent** for sensitive personal information (e.g., health, financial, government-issued IDs). Consent must be:
- Requested for a specific purpose
- Given separately for each purpose
- Not bundled with other consents
- Capable of being revoked at any time

### 9.2 Individual Rights (Phase 2 — September 2023)

| Right                             | Description                                                                                    |
|-----------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Access**                        | Right to access personal information held by the organisation                                  |
| **Correction**                    | Right to have inaccurate information corrected                                                 |
| **Portability**                   | Right to receive personal information in a structured, technological format (data portability) |
| **Deletion**                      | Right to have information deleted in prescribed circumstances                                  |
| **De-indexing**                   | Right to have hyperlinks linking to personal information de-indexed                            |
| **Object to automated decisions** | Right to be informed of automated decisions and request human review                           |

---

## 10. Automated Decision-Making

Law 25 includes **specific requirements for automated decision-making** — particularly relevant for FRFIs using AI/ML models:

- Organisations must **disclose** that a decision based solely on automated processing is being made
- Individuals have the right to **request human review** of decisions affecting their rights
- Organisations must **explain** the factors and parameters used in the automated decision

This provision directly intersects with OSFI E-23 (model risk management) and OSFI expectations for explainable AI.

## 11. Interaction with PIPEDA and OSFI Obligations

| Obligation                 | PIPEDA DERR                     | Quebec Law 25                       | Which Governs for FRFIs                          |
|----------------------------|---------------------------------|-------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| Breach reporting threshold | "Real risk of significant harm" | "Risk of serious injury"            | Both — substantively similar; meet both          |
| Reporting recipient        | OPC                             | CAI                                 | Both — different recipients                      |
| **Reporting timeline**     | "As soon as feasible"           | **72 hours**                        | **Law 25 is stricter — 72 hours governs Quebec** |
| Individual notification    | As soon as feasible             | As soon as possible                 | Both — substantively similar                     |
| Record-keeping period      | 24 months                       | Not specified — ongoing             | At least 24 months (PIPEDA minimum)              |
| PIA requirement            | Not mandated                    | Mandatory for new systems/transfers | Law 25 PIA required for Quebec scope             |
| Portability                | Not yet mandated (PIPEDA)       | Mandatory (Phase 2)                 | Law 25 governs                                   |

### PIPEDA and Law 25 Concurrency

When an incident involves Quebec residents' personal information:
- **Both PIPEDA DERR and Law 25 apply**
- The **72-hour CAI window** is the binding constraint (stricter than PIPEDA)
- FRFIs must notify **both the OPC and the CAI** where the incident triggers both thresholds
- Individual notification must satisfy the requirements of both regimes

## 12. Relevance to OSFI 24-Hour Incident Reporting

### 12.1 The 72-Hour CAI Window vs. the 24-Hour OSFI Window

The Law 25 72-hour CAI reporting window is **less strict** than the OSFI Advisory's 24-hour window. For incidents involving Quebec personal information:

| Clock             | Starts At             | Deadline            | Recipient                                |
|-------------------|-----------------------|---------------------|------------------------------------------|
| **OSFI Advisory** | Detection of incident | **24 hours**        | OSFI TRD + Lead Supervisor               |
| **Law 25**        | Awareness of incident | **72 hours**        | Commission d'accès à l'information (CAI) |
| **PIPEDA DERR**   | Breach determination  | As soon as feasible | OPC                                      |

**OSFI notification will occur first.** FRFIs should not sequence these obligations as OSFI → Law 25; they run in parallel, and the OSFI deadline is the binding constraint.

### 12.2 CAI Notification Triggers OSFI Advisory Criterion C3

Under Criterion C3 of the OSFI Advisory, reporting to "any local or foreign regulatory or supervisory agencies" is a standalone incident reporting trigger. This includes the CAI.

> 📌 **Once a FRFI determines it must notify the CAI under Law 25, it must also notify OSFI** (if not already done). However, since OSFI's 24-hour window will typically fire first, OSFI should be notified before CAI notification is submitted.

### 12.3 Quebec Incidents May Have Systemic Risk Implications

For FRFIs with large Quebec customer bases (e.g., major Canadian banks, Desjardins, Quebec-chartered insurers), a Quebec-resident data breach may affect a large number of customers — directly activating:
- Criterion A5 (confidentiality/integrity/availability of customer information)
- Criterion D2 (growing number of external customers impacted)
- Criterion D1 (high/critical severity due to scale)

### 12.4 PIA Failures as Incident Precursors

Law 25's PIA requirement means that IT projects and third-party arrangements involving Quebec data should have undergone privacy risk assessment. If an incident occurs on a system for which no PIA was conducted (or where PIA-identified risks were not mitigated), this may:
- Represent a compliance failure reportable to the CAI
- Indicate a gap in the FRFI's B-13 data management (P10) or change management (P8) controls
- Feature in the OSFI post-incident review as a contributing factor

> 📌 **Summary:** Quebec Law 25 creates a **72-hour CAI reporting obligation** for confidentiality incidents involving Quebec residents' personal data — an obligation that runs concurrently with the OSFI 24-hour window. CAI notification is itself a Criterion C3 OSFI trigger. FRFIs must integrate Law 25 compliance into their incident response and OSFI reporting procedures.
