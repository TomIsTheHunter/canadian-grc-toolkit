## 1. Overview

OSFI Guideline B-10 establishes the framework for how FRFIs must manage risks arising from their use of **third-party arrangements** — any contractual relationship where a third party performs activities, functions, or services on behalf of, or in support of, a FRFI.

The 2024 iteration of B-10 represents a fundamental expansion from the predecessor 2001 *Outsourcing Guideline*, moving from a compliance-based to a **risk-based, outcomes-focused** model that reflects the modern reality of cloud computing, fintech partnerships, data sharing arrangements, and complex vendor ecosystems.

### Key Principles

- FRFIs **remain fully accountable** to OSFI for all activities performed by third parties on their behalf
- Third-party risk management is a **board and senior management responsibility**
- The level of oversight must be **proportional to risk** — specifically to the criticality of the arrangement and the sensitivity of data involved
- Concentration risk at both the FRFI and sector levels must be actively managed

---

## 2. Scope — Beyond Outsourcing

### 2.1 What Constitutes a Third-Party Arrangement

B-10 applies to **all arrangements** where a FRFI relies on a third party, including but not limited to:

| Arrangement Type                         | Examples                                                               |
|------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Technology and Cloud Services**        | IaaS, PaaS, SaaS providers; co-location data centre providers          |
| **Business Process Outsourcing**         | Call centres, payment processing, claims administration                |
| **Professional and Advisory Services**   | Legal, audit, consulting (where access to systems or data is provided) |
| **Data Services**                        | Credit bureaus, market data providers, fraud analytics platforms       |
| **Fintech and Partnership Arrangements** | Open banking integrations, embedded finance partnerships               |
| **Intra-group Arrangements**             | Services received from parent companies, affiliates, or subsidiaries   |

### 2.2 What is Excluded

The following are generally outside the scope of B-10:
- Arrangements where the third party has **no access to FRFI systems, data, or operations**
- Publicly available utilities (e.g., standard power or telecommunications services)
- Transactions conducted at arm's length in the normal course of business (e.g., securities trades)

## 3. Risk-Based Approach and Arrangement Criticality

### 3.1 The Criticality Framework

B-10 introduces a **criticality assessment** as the cornerstone of proportional oversight. FRFIs must assess each third-party arrangement based on:

| Assessment Dimension        | Considerations                                                                                            |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Operational Criticality** | Would failure or disruption of this arrangement impact the FRFI's ability to perform critical operations? |
| **Data Sensitivity**        | Does the arrangement involve access to personal information, financial data, or other sensitive data?     |
| **Substitutability**        | How quickly and easily could the FRFI replace this third party?                                           |
| **Concentration**           | Is there reliance on a single provider for multiple critical services?                                    |
| **Interconnectedness**      | Does this third party connect to other FRFIs or financial market infrastructure?                          |

### 3.2 Arrangement Tiers

Based on criticality and risk assessment, arrangements are categorised as:

| Tier          | Description                                                                                         | Enhanced Requirements                            |
|---------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------|
| **Critical**  | Supports critical operations; failure would cause severe disruption to the FRFI or financial system | Most stringent — see Section 5                   |
| **High-Risk** | Significant risk exposure (data sensitivity, operational dependency) but below critical threshold   | Enhanced due diligence, monitoring               |
| **Standard**  | Lower risk; limited operational or data exposure                                                    | Baseline contractual and monitoring requirements |

## 4. Lifecycle Requirements

B-10 establishes requirements across the **full lifecycle** of a third-party arrangement:

### 4.1 Pre-Arrangement Due Diligence
- Risk assessment and criticality classification prior to contracting
- Evaluation of the third party's financial soundness, operational resilience, and security posture
- Concentration risk assessment (sector-wide and FRFI-specific)
- Legal and regulatory risk assessment (e.g., jurisdiction, data sovereignty)

### 4.2 Contracting
- Minimum contractual provisions per Annex 2 (see Section 6)
- Right-to-audit and access clauses
- Incident notification obligations embedded in contracts

### 4.3 Ongoing Monitoring
- Performance monitoring against agreed service levels
- Regular security and compliance assessments for high-risk and critical arrangements
- Monitoring of subcontractor (fourth-party) risk
- Continuous review of concentration exposure

### 4.4 Exit and Transition Planning
- Documented exit strategies for all critical and high-risk arrangements
- Regular testing of exit and transition plans
- Data portability and return/destruction requirements

## 5. High-Risk and Critical Arrangement Requirements

For arrangements classified as **high-risk or critical**, B-10 requires additional measures:

| Requirement                            | Description                                                                                                                              |
|----------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Enhanced Due Diligence**             | Deep-dive assessment of the third party's resilience, security controls, and sub-contractor dependencies                                 |
| **Board / Senior Management Approval** | New or materially changed critical arrangements require board or designated executive approval                                           |
| **On-site Assessments**                | FRFI retains the right and obligation to conduct periodic on-site assessments or commission independent audits                           |
| **Business Continuity Testing**        | Joint BCP/DR testing with the third party; FRFI must validate that the third party can meet recovery objectives                          |
| **Concentration Risk Monitoring**      | Active monitoring of FRFI-level and sector-level concentration exposure                                                                  |
| **Exit Planning**                      | Documented, tested exit strategy with defined RTO/RPO for substitution or in-sourcing                                                    |
| **Regulatory Notification**            | OSFI expects FRFIs to notify their Lead Supervisor when entering into, materially changing, or exiting critical third-party arrangements |

## 6. Minimum Contractual Provisions — Annex 2

B-10 Annex 2 specifies **minimum contractual provisions** that must be included in all high-risk and critical arrangements, and considered for all other arrangements:

| Provision Category                 | Key Requirements                                                                                                                                                              |
|------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Scope of Services**              | Clear definition of services, deliverables, and performance standards                                                                                                         |
| **Data Ownership and Security**    | FRFI retains data ownership; third party must implement security controls meeting FRFI standards                                                                              |
| **Audit Rights**                   | FRFI (and OSFI) right to audit, inspect, and review third party's controls and performance                                                                                    |
| **Incident Notification**          | Third party must notify FRFI of any security incident, disaster declaration, or material service disruption **promptly** — specific timelines should be contractually defined |
| **Sub-contracting**                | FRFI approval required for sub-contractors that have access to FRFI data or systems; sub-contractors must meet equivalent standards                                           |
| **Business Continuity**            | Third party must maintain and test BCP/DR plans meeting the FRFI's recovery objectives                                                                                        |
| **Regulatory Access**              | OSFI and other relevant regulators must have access to the third party's premises and records                                                                                 |
| **Termination Rights**             | FRFI must retain the right to terminate for cause, including material security incidents or regulatory non-compliance                                                         |
| **Data Return/Destruction**        | Upon termination, third party must return or securely destroy all FRFI data                                                                                                   |
| **Jurisdiction and Governing Law** | Specify governing law and jurisdiction; address cross-border data transfer risks                                                                                              |

## 7. Third-Party Incident Notification Chains

### 7.1 Contractual Notification Obligations

A core requirement of B-10 is that **third parties must notify the FRFI** of incidents that affect the FRFI's data or operations. Contracts must specify:

- **What triggers notification:** Security incidents, data breaches, disaster declarations, material service disruptions, regulatory actions against the third party
- **Notification timelines:** B-10 recommends contractually requiring third-party notification within timeframes that enable the FRFI to meet its own regulatory reporting obligations (e.g., within 4–8 hours to support a 24-hour OSFI notification window)
- **Notification format:** Initial verbal/electronic notice followed by written confirmation
- **Escalation chain:** Designated contacts at both the third party and FRFI

### 7.2 FRFI Responsibility to Flow Up

Upon receiving notification from a third party, the FRFI must:

1. **Assess the incident** against OSFI incident reporting trigger criteria (including B-group criteria in the Incident Reporting Advisory)
2. **Escalate internally** per the FRFI's incident response protocol
3. **Report to OSFI** if the incident meets reporting thresholds — the FRFI cannot delegate this obligation to the third party

> ⚠️ **Accountability Reminder:** Even if a third party caused the incident, the **FRFI is responsible** for reporting to OSFI. OSFI does not have a direct reporting relationship with third-party vendors.

### 7.3 Disaster Declarations

Under the OSFI Incident Reporting Advisory, a **disaster declaration by a third-party vendor that impacts the FRFI** is an explicit reporting trigger (Criterion B5). FRFIs must monitor for disaster declarations from critical and high-risk third parties and ensure contractual notification obligations cover this scenario.

## 8. Concentration and Fourth-Party Risk

### 8.1 Concentration Risk

B-10 requires FRFIs to monitor and manage concentration risk at multiple levels:

| Concentration Type             | Description                                                                                      |
|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **FRFI-level concentration**   | Over-reliance on a single third party for multiple critical services                             |
| **Sector-level concentration** | Multiple FRFIs relying on the same third party — systemic risk implications                      |
| **Geographic concentration**   | Reliance on third parties in a single jurisdiction (e.g., natural disaster or geopolitical risk) |
| **Technology concentration**   | Over-reliance on a single technology platform or cloud provider                                  |

### 8.2 Fourth-Party Risk

FRFIs must understand and manage risks from their third parties' own subcontractors (fourth parties):

- Maintain visibility into critical subcontractors used by critical and high-risk third parties
- Contractually require third parties to notify the FRFI of material changes to their subcontractor arrangements
- Assess the cumulative concentration risk created by fourth-party dependencies

## 9. Relevance to OSFI 24-Hour Incident Reporting

OSFI B-10 directly intersects with the OSFI Technology and Cybersecurity Incident Reporting Advisory in several critical ways:

### 9.1 Third-Party Incidents as Reporting Triggers

The Incident Reporting Advisory includes multiple **B-group trigger criteria** that arise directly from third-party arrangements:

| Advisory Criterion                                                       | B-10 Connection                                                                            |
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **B4** — Disaster recovery plans activated                               | B-10 requires DR plans; DR activation (including at a third party) triggers reporting      |
| **B5** — Disaster declaration by a third-party vendor impacting the FRFI | Direct contractual obligation — third parties must notify FRFIs; FRFIs must report to OSFI |
| **B6** — Impact to a third party affecting the FRFI                      | Broader third-party operational incidents (not just formal disaster declarations)          |
| **A4** — Impact to FRFI operations, infrastructure, data, and/or systems | Third-party-caused disruptions to FRFI operations are fully in scope                       |

### 9.2 Contractual Notification Timelines Must Support the 24-Hour Window

B-10's Annex 2 contractual provisions require incident notification clauses. These contractual timelines must be calibrated to ensure the FRFI receives third-party notification **early enough** to conduct its own assessment and submit the OSFI report within 24 hours.

> 📌 **Best Practice:** Negotiate third-party incident notification clauses requiring notification within **2–4 hours** of the third party detecting an incident that affects FRFI systems or data. This supports both the organisational 12-hour internal escalation threshold and the 24-hour OSFI regulatory deadline.

### 9.3 FRFI Cannot Delegate Reporting Obligation

Regardless of the contractual structure with a third party, the **FRFI bears sole responsibility** for OSFI incident reporting. B-10's accountability principle reinforces this: FRFIs remain accountable to OSFI for all outsourced and third-party activities.

### 9.4 Governance Trigger — Board Escalation (C2)

Under B-10, material third-party incidents affecting critical arrangements require escalation to the Board or Senior Management. The Incident Reporting Advisory Criterion C2 (incident reported to the Board or Senior/Executive Management) is itself an OSFI reporting trigger. These obligations are therefore mutually reinforcing.
