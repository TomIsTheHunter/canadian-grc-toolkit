## 1. Overview

OSFI Guideline E-21 establishes the framework for **operational risk management and operational resilience** across all FRFIs. The August 2024 revision significantly expands the guideline's scope beyond traditional operational risk management to encompass a **resilience-first** approach — the expectation that FRFIs can absorb, adapt, and recover from operational disruptions while continuing to deliver critical services.

### Core Principle

> **Operational resilience** is the ability of a FRFI to deliver critical operations through disruption. This is not merely about surviving incidents — it requires demonstrating that critical services remain available to customers, counterparties, and the financial system even under severe but plausible stress scenarios.

### Key Areas Covered

- Identification and protection of **critical operations**
- **Impact tolerances** for critical operations
- Scenario analysis including technology failures, cyberattacks, and third-party disruptions
- Operational risk **loss data collection** and reporting
- Governance of operational risk across the three lines of defence

## 2. Phased Implementation Timeline

The August 2024 E-21 guideline introduced expanded operational resilience requirements with a phased compliance schedule:

| Phase       | Deadline              | Requirements                                                                                                     |
|-------------|-----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Phase 1** | August 2024           | Initial publication; FRFIs begin gap assessment and implementation planning                                      |
| **Phase 2** | Early 2025            | Identification of critical operations and preliminary impact tolerance setting                                   |
| **Phase 3** | **September 1, 2026** | **Full adherence required** — all operational resilience, scenario testing, and reporting requirements in effect |

> ⚠️ **Note (April 2026):** Full adherence to the updated E-21 is required by **September 1, 2026**. FRFIs should be in advanced stages of implementation at this date.

The phased timeline was designed to align with the implementation timelines for **OSFI B-10** (May 1, 2024) and **OSFI B-13** (January 1, 2024), recognising that all three guidelines form an integrated suite.

## 3. Operational Resilience Framework

### 3.1 Resilience vs. Traditional Risk Management

| Traditional Operational Risk   | Operational Resilience                                |
|--------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| Focus on *preventing* failures | Focus on *continuing to operate* through failures     |
| Risk assessment and mitigation | Stress testing continuity of critical operations      |
| Controls reduce probability    | Impact tolerances define acceptable disruption limits |
| Backward-looking (loss data)   | Forward-looking (scenario analysis)                   |

### 3.2 Impact Tolerances

A core E-21 requirement is that FRFIs define **impact tolerances** for each critical operation — the maximum level of disruption (in terms of duration and customer/market impact) that the FRFI would accept before outcomes become unacceptable.

Impact tolerances must address:
- **Duration:** Maximum tolerable outage duration for a critical operation
- **Volume:** Maximum number of transactions or customers affected
- **Breadth:** Geographic scope of acceptable disruption
- **Velocity:** Rate at which disruption can be remediated

FRFIs must demonstrate — through scenario testing — that they can remain within their impact tolerances under severe but plausible disruption scenarios.

## 4. Critical Operations

### 4.1 What Are Critical Operations?

E-21 defines **critical operations** as those whose disruption would:

- Cause significant harm to customers or counterparties
- Impair the FRFI's ability to maintain financial soundness
- Pose a risk to financial stability (systemic significance)
- Result in significant reputational damage

### 4.2 Identifying Critical Operations

FRFIs must conduct a **Business Services Analysis (BSA)** or equivalent to identify critical operations:

| Step                      | Activity                                                                                        |
|---------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Map services**          | Identify all services provided to customers and the financial system                            |
| **Assess criticality**    | Evaluate impact if the service were disrupted — financial, reputational, systemic               |
| **Identify dependencies** | Map underlying processes, technology, data, people, and third parties that support each service |
| **Set impact tolerances** | Define the maximum tolerable disruption for each critical operation                             |
| **Test continuity**       | Scenario test against severe but plausible disruptions                                          |

### 4.3 Examples of Typically Critical Operations

- Payment initiation and processing (retail and wholesale)
- Settlement and clearing connectivity
- Core banking ledger processing
- Customer authentication and account access
- Trade execution and confirmation
- Regulatory reporting (including to OSFI)

## 5. Operational Risk Management

Beyond operational resilience, E-21 maintains strong requirements for **traditional operational risk management**:

### 5.1 Risk Identification and Assessment

- Comprehensive operational risk identification across all business lines and processes
- Risk and Control Self-Assessment (RCSA) or equivalent
- Scenario analysis for low-frequency, high-impact events (including cyberattacks, natural disasters, pandemics)

### 5.2 Loss Data Collection

- Collection and analysis of internal operational risk loss data
- Classification by Basel II/III operational risk event categories, including:
  - **Execution, Delivery and Process Management** (technology failures)
  - **Business Disruption and System Failures** (cyberattacks, outages)
  - **Clients, Products and Business Practices** (fraud)
- Loss data feeds into capital modelling (for FRFIs using Advanced Measurement Approach) and risk management reporting

### 5.3 Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

- Defined KRIs for operational risks, including technology and cyber metrics
- Regular monitoring and Board/Senior Management reporting on KRI thresholds and breaches
- **KRI threshold breaches may constitute an OSFI incident reporting trigger** (Criterion D4 — breach of internal risk appetite/thresholds)

## 6. Scenario Analysis and Stress Testing

E-21 requires FRFIs to conduct **scenario analysis** to test operational resilience. Scenarios must include:

| Scenario Category        | Examples Relevant to Cyber/Technology                                            |
|--------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Cyber Attack**         | Ransomware attack on core systems; DDoS attack on customer-facing platforms      |
| **Technology Failure**   | Critical system failure (mainframe, cloud provider outage); data centre loss     |
| **Third-Party Failure**  | Critical vendor insolvency or service disruption; cloud provider extended outage |
| **Pandemic / Workforce** | Mass staff unavailability; remote work infrastructure failure                    |
| **Concurrent Events**    | Technology failure simultaneously with high-volume trading period                |

Scenario testing must demonstrate that the FRFI can:
1. Continue critical operations within impact tolerances
2. Execute recovery procedures effectively
3. Communicate with customers, regulators, and counterparties
4. Return to normal operations within acceptable timelines

## 7. Proportionality Principle

E-21 explicitly applies the **proportionality principle** — implementation requirements scale with the size, complexity, and risk profile of the FRFI:

| FRFI Characteristics       | Implementation Expectations                                                                                         |
|----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **D-SIBs and large FRFIs** | Comprehensive resilience framework; board-level resilience oversight; extensive scenario testing programme          |
| **Mid-size FRFIs**         | Documented critical operations and impact tolerances; regular scenario testing; dedicated operational risk function |
| **Small FRFIs**            | Simplified approach; core resilience capabilities; proportionate governance                                         |

Regardless of size, all FRFIs must identify at least their critical operations and demonstrate continuity capability.

## 8. Relationship to B-10 and B-13

E-21 is the **overarching operational risk and resilience framework** within which B-10 and B-13 operate as domain-specific guidelines:

```
E-21 (Operational Risk & Resilience — Overarching Framework)
    │
    ├── B-13 (Technology & Cyber Risk Management — Technology Domain)
    │        ├── Technology Operations & Resilience (P6–P11)
    │        └── Cybersecurity (P12–P16)
    │
    └── B-10 (Third-Party Risk Management — Third-Party Domain)
             ├── Critical arrangement identification
             └── Third-party resilience and continuity
```

| Integration Point                          | Description                                                                                                             |
|--------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Critical Operations ↔ Critical Systems** | E-21 critical operations identification drives B-13's Critical System Register; the two registers must be aligned       |
| **Impact Tolerances ↔ RTO/RPO**            | E-21 impact tolerances set the requirement; B-13 P9 BCP/DR sets the technical RTO/RPO to meet those tolerances          |
| **Third-Party Dependencies**               | E-21 requires critical operation dependency mapping; B-10 governs the contracts and monitoring for those third parties  |
| **Scenario Testing**                       | E-21 scenario requirements are operationalised through B-13 P9 (BCP/DR testing) and B-10 third-party resilience testing |

## 9. Relevance to OSFI 24-Hour Incident Reporting

### 9.1 Operational Disruptions Are Reportable Events

E-21 operational risk events — particularly in the **Business Disruption and System Failures** category — directly map to OSFI Incident Reporting Advisory trigger criteria:

| E-21 Operational Risk Category                 | Incident Advisory Trigger                            |
|------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------|
| Business disruption and system failures        | B1 (disruptions to business systems/operations)      |
| Technology failures causing operational impact | A4 (impact to FRFI operations, infrastructure, data) |
| Critical operation continuity breach           | A6 (operational impact to key/critical systems)      |
| Third-party disruption                         | B5, B6 (third-party incidents affecting the FRFI)    |

### 9.2 KRI Breach as a Reporting Trigger

E-21 requires FRFIs to monitor operational risk KRIs. The Incident Reporting Advisory Criterion **D4** (breach of internal risk appetite or thresholds) means that a **KRI threshold breach classified as an operational risk event is itself a reporting trigger** under the Advisory.

### 9.3 Impact Tolerance Breach

An incident that causes a critical operation to exceed its defined **impact tolerance** is a high-severity event under E-21. This typically corresponds to:
- Advisory Criterion D1 (FRFI-assessed high or critical severity)
- Advisory Criterion A6 (operational impact to key/critical systems)
- Advisory Criterion A4 (impact to FRFI operations)

### 9.4 Scenario Testing Must Include Regulatory Notification

E-21 scenario testing must be comprehensive. Best practice is for scenarios to include **regulatory notification as a step** — testing the organisation's ability to meet the 24-hour OSFI notification requirement is itself part of validating operational resilience.
